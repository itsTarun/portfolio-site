# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mobile-focused clients, startup CTOs, and engineering leads who need iOS or Flutter expertise. Also: potential full-time employers, OSS collaborators, and developers who discovered Tarun through writing or a project. They arrive with varying levels of technical depth — some want to verify capability before sending a hire inquiry; others are peers evaluating craft. Common context: opening the site on mobile to check out someone they just heard about, or desk-first review during a hiring search.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Tarun Sharma — a mobile developer with 7+ years building iOS and Flutter apps (2018–present), specializing in architecture, release quality, and R&D. The site's job is threefold with roughly equal weight: attract freelance/contract clients, signal credibility to full-time employers, and build an ongoing public presence for writing and open-source work. Success looks like someone arriving uncertain and leaving with no remaining doubt about capability and style.

## Brand Personality

Precise, Reliable, Bold. The same qualities Tarun builds into his apps — stable releases, solid architecture, hands-on QA — should be self-evident in the site itself. The design earns trust the way good software does: by doing exactly what it promises, with nothing superfluous.

## Anti-references

- **Generic dev resume sites**: Avatar + 3 skill cards + contact form. Forgettable by design.
- **SaaS startup landing pages**: Gradient blobs, cream/off-white backgrounds, manufactured social proof. Positions the portfolio as a product to sell rather than work to show.
- **Dark hacker aesthetic**: Terminal green-on-black, monospace everything, ASCII art. Trades craft for cosplay.
- **Minimal design portfolio**: All whitespace and thin Helvetica — no texture, no weight, no presence. Feels like a designer's lookbook, not a builder's body of work.

## Design Principles

1. **Build as evidence** — design choices should demonstrate the same qualities as the apps: precision, restraint, and nothing breaking. The site should feel like software that ships.
2. **Bold without aggression** — the neo-brutalist vocabulary (hard borders, offset shadows, tight radius) signals authority and intention, not hostility. Force without loudness.
3. **Work earns the page** — projects, writing, and the timeline are the product. No section exists to pad the visual weight; every element has a job.
4. **Direct, not decorative** — copy is confident and specific. No hedging adjectives, no "passionate about building amazing experiences." State facts.
5. **Reliability is visible** — speed, accessibility, and graceful fallbacks aren't afterthoughts. A 99% crash-free rate claim means nothing if the page lags or breaks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Dark and light mode both first-class (system-default). Reduced motion support required for all animations. No reliance on color alone for conveying meaning.
